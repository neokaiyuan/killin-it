Introduction

Our motivation is that note-taking in reading classes is redundant. Notetaking is a time consuming process, and many people take similar notes. It would be more efficient for everyone to take notes on the same document, such that everyone would be able to benefit from each other without face to face communication.

The goal is to simplify the group reading process by allowing everyone to share the same copy of a text. This would a) reduce redundancy in note taking, and b) provide people long-lasting notes and memories of their discussions.

For this week of user-testing, we are splitting our tests into two phrases: 1. lab study and 2. deployment. After our lab study, we will evaluate our design with performance measures and semi-structured interviews, and adjust our design accordingly for the deployment phase. We will evaluate our deployment study with performance measures, questionnares and surveys, and semi-structured interviews.

Methods

Recruitment

  • We recruited students in humanities classes
  • We recruited them by introducing them to our product at a time when they had a PDF to read for class
  • We used their PDF as the document in our sample

Study Design

    lab study (in person)
  • performance measures
  • ask for completion of tasks
    • read text
    • annotate section
    • view annotations
    • respond to annotation
  • thinking aloud
  • interview
    • how often do students read PDF texts?
    • given a choice between a PDF text and a hard copy, which would you choose to read?
    • how do students take notes on a text today?
    • how do students communicate about a text today?
    • how would you use this product?
    • how does this compare to manually writing notes and discussing them in class, versus writing notes on a device that are shared by the class?
  • survey / questionnaire
    • how well does this tool allow students to take notes on a text?
    • how well does this tool allow students to collaborate on a text?
    • how useful would this be for class?
  • deployment
    • performance measures (number of recordings, length of recordings, number of views, number of responses, numbers of errors)
    • semi-structured interview (same as above)
    • exit survey (same as above)
